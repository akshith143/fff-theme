Food, Hunger and Shifting norms during COVID-19

The immediate impact of the pandemic is felt through the weakening of national food demands and the closure of many businesses such as caterers and hotels, which led to excess of food production by local farmers.

There are nearly 690 million people in the world who are hungry, or 8.9 per cent of the world population – up 10 million people in one year and nearly 60 million in five years and the COVID19 pandemic has only exacerbated the problem.
